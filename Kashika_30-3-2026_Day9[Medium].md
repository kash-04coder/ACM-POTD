Here the ideal is to find the position where the circular linked list starts to loop. Basically the element which is repeated a second time foremost (firstly). The point at which the loop starts. We may use a hashmap to store the nodes and their occurances and keep in check when and which node's occurance turn from once to twice. Tht particular node would be the one that is the starting of loop.
We can also use the hare and tortoise algorithm in this case. First we find out whether the given linked list is a circular LL or not. For this, we assign 2 variables, namely fast and slow. Slow iterates one by one while fast moves two at a time. When slow is equal to fast for the first time, that indicates the presence of circular LL. Resetting slow to head and again iterating the loop would give us the starting point of the loop as it is in accordance with the stepping/increment of the slow and fast pointers.
